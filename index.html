LEGO EV3 Robotics Sensors Automation

LEGO Robotics (EV3) - Level 1
Futurite, South City Centre, Kolkata

Built and programmed EV3-based robots as an early hands-on introduction to robotics and automation. The course sparked a long-term interest in technology, mechanical design, programming logic, and practical problem-solving.

  • Reptar - IR Sensor Based Snake Robot: A snake-like EV3 robot that used infrared sensing to detect nearby objects and trigger a sudden jump or strike motion, combining mechanical linkage design with reactive sensor logic.
  • Robotic Arm - EV3 Sensor-Based Arm: A multi-jointed robotic arm with a sensor-equipped head and gripper mechanism, exploring robotic motion, sensor integration, and mechanical articulation.
  • EV3 Forklift Model: A forklift-style robot capable of lifting and moving objects through a motorized lever mechanism, applying mechanical leverage and motor control concepts.
  • Hybrid Walker - Wheel & Stilt-Based Robot: A four-legged robot combining wheels with stilt-like leg structures to explore locomotion mechanics and animal-inspired movement.
  • LEGO Mindstorms Electric Guitar: A motion-responsive EV3 instrument that used an ultrasonic sensor to measure distance and adjust sound frequency dynamically.
  • Dancer - Color Sensor-Based Dancing Robot: A humanoid-style robot that detected colors and responded with corresponding dance movements through motor-controlled choreography.
  • EV3 Robotic Saw: A handheld robotic saw model with a motorized cutting arm, exploring tool-based automation and motor-driven mechanical motion.
  • EV3 Scissor Lift Mechanism: A motorized scissor lift that extended vertically using linkage principles and motor-driven actuation.
  • EV3 Automatic Gate with Traffic Control: A Class 6 vehicle-and-gate simulation using sensor-triggered motion and logical sequencing in EV3 block-based software.
  • Gravity-Powered Wind Turbine Model: A turbine model that converted gravitational potential energy into rotational blade motion without motor input, foreshadowing later interest in gravity-based energy systems.
